03 · Usage

Clear space & minimum size.

Breathing room equal to the height of the "S". Keep at least that much clear space between the mark and any other element — type, photography, borders. At small sizes, don't go below 24 px for digital or 8 mm for print — the ring and interlocked letters begin to muddy below that.
Do
  • Use on warm neutral backgrounds (stone, linen, off-white) or on deep forest / charcoal.
  • Use the monochrome forest or charcoal variants when single-colour printing.
  • Use the on-dark variant when the background is darker than 40% grey.
  • Keep the horizontal lockup as the default for letterheads, email, and web.
Don't
  • Don't stretch, skew, or rotate the mark or lockup.
  • Don't recolour individual letters — the S stays forest-green, the D stays terracotta.
  • Don't place the mark on busy photography without a darkening overlay.
  • Don't use the wordmark alone unless the mark has been shown on the same page.
  • Don't add drop shadows, bevels, or outlines to the mark.

06 · Voice

How we sound.

Trustworthy, grounded, premium-practical. Like an established estate-management firm — not corporate and cold, not rustic or folksy. We write the way we work: plainly, directly, without exaggeration.

Grounded

We describe what we actually do. "We replaced the guttering last Tuesday" beats "delivering best-in-class property solutions."

Quietly premium

Taste and precision are shown, not claimed. The typography, the colour, the photography already say "premium" — the words can stay simple.

Practitioner's tone

Names the materials, the techniques, the climate constraints. "Kikuyu lawn," "Highveld dry season," "aluminium scaffold" — specificity signals expertise.

Accountable

Short, concrete sentences. Never promise what the crew can't deliver. "We'll be on site Tuesday 8am" beats "we look forward to partnering with you."
