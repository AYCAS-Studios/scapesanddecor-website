The brief

A sanctuary that had earned a refresh.

The Celebration Centre auditorium is the main gathering space for one of Harare's largest congregations — 3,500 seats across a tiered floor and a sweeping upper balcony. Weekly use, stage lighting, and the scale of the room itself had, over time, dulled the finish across walls and columns.

We were brought in to repaint the entire interior: walls, structural columns, ceiling transitions and architectural trim. The brief was straightforward but demanding: deliver a crisp, evenly-finished auditorium within a fixed window — without disrupting the church's ordinary weekly rhythm, and without leaving a single drip on the fixed seating, carpeting, fittings or AV rigging.

On site

The work in progress.

Full-height internal scaffolding across the seating tiers. Full floor and fixture protection. A disciplined spray application on walls and columns, with brush and roller on trim and edges.

How we ran the job

From access to hand-over.

01—

Scoped & specified

Full-height survey, substrate assessment, paint-system specification and a written schedule — agreed with church leadership before a single coat.

02—

Accessed & protected

Internal scaffolding across the seating tiers. Full carpet, seating, AV rigging and fixture protection — masked, wrapped and drop-clothed before prep started.

03—

Prepared the substrate

Sanding, filling, crack repair and priming — the unglamorous majority of the on-site time. No shortcuts on prep means no flaking in year two.

04—

Applied the finish

Spray application on wall and column expanses; brush and roller on trim, edges and architectural detail. Coat schedule respected end-to-end.

05—

Closed out & handed over

Scaffold struck, site swept, protection removed, snag walk-through with the project sponsor. A touch-up kit left on-site for future maintenance.

Large or small — the same discipline.

A single feature wall or a full auditorium — we inspect the space, specify the paint system and quote plainly. Free site visit, fully insured crews, and a written schedule before a coat goes on.
